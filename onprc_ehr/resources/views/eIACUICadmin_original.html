eIACUC Administration

The primary purpose of the Electronic Institutional Animal Care and Use Committee (eIACUC) is to automate and streamline the protocol submission and review process for animal research. This web-based system also handles other federally mandated IACUC functions, such as facility inspections and meetings1.

 

  • At Oregon Health and Science University (OHSU), the eIACUC system is used to manage all aspects of animal care and use in research and teaching. This includes protocol submission, review, and approval processes. The system ensures compliance with the Animal Welfare Act regulations and Public Health Service (PHS) Policy

    OHSU’s eIACUC system also supports facility inspections, policy updates, and training for researchers. It helps maintain high standards of animal welfare and ensures that all research involving animals is conducted ethically and responsibly2.

     

    The primary purpose of the PRIMe application at the Oregon National Primate Research Center (ONPRC) is to manage and streamline the research processes involving nonhuman primates. This application helps in tracking and documenting research protocols, ensuring compliance with regulatory standards, and facilitating efficient communication among researchers.
